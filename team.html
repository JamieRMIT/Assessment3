Career Plans

Adrien




Christopher

My career plans have less changed in ways I would not have imagined since the beginning of the prerequisite introductory courses due to a range of factors. Prior to the commencement the Introduction to IT and Introduction to Programming, I didn’t envisage programming being as difficult to comprehend as it is. This does not mean that I have or will give up on it but my inability to do so means that at this time it is in my best interest to pursue a different career, instead I will begin studying for the Bachelor of Arts majoring in Japanese, which I am already fluent in with the hope to move on to a translation course upon its completion. I hope that while I am studying for the Bachelor of Arts (Japanese) I will be able to study programming at my own pace and perhaps try again in a few years’ time as a second degree.

I still have a vested interest in technology and the Introduction to IT course in particular has definitely whetted my appetite. I have had an absolute blast working with my teammates and will cherish the time with them for years to come. While working on the project, I was taken aback by how enamoured some members were with my project idea, it being chosen for our group project, that one member when as far as to build a prototype of it. If we were able to go into business with this idea and actually make some real money with it, it would make this whole endeavour worthwhile.

In conclusion, thanks to this course, even though I have chosen to drop it and study for a completely different degree next semester, I now have a range of options open to me. I can go into business with my group members with the E-Ink Car Display while pursuing a career in translation.
